## Design Context

### Users
TadLock is for people who tend to eat while distracted by their phones. They use the app during meals when they want help blocking distractions and staying present while they eat. The core job to be done is to reduce phone-driven interruption long enough to let the user finish a meal with more focus and less compulsive scrolling.

### Brand Personality
Commitment, dedication, fun. TadLock should feel emotionally safe: supportive, steady, and encouraging rather than shaming, punitive, or severe. The product can feel playful through Tad and small moments of warmth, but it should still communicate reliability and self-control.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the current theme direction and treat the existing modes as valid first-class experiences. The visual tone should feel calm, focused, and mobile-native, with clear hierarchy, strong readability, and touch-friendly controls. No external reference products were provided. Avoid generic productivity SaaS styling, harsh "phone jail" aesthetics, and overly noisy card-on-card layouts that make the product feel cluttered or punitive.

### Design Principles
1. Support focused eating, not punishment.
2. Make the interface feel emotionally safe and easy to trust.
3. Use playful warmth with restraint so the app stays credible.
4. Prioritize clear hierarchy, large touch targets, and in-the-moment mobile usability.
5. Keep theme behavior consistent across surfaces, states, and flows, with a practical accessibility baseline by default.
